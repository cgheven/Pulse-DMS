  Al-Hasan Molty Form | Dealer Management System

This guide walks through every section of the system with
real examples based on a Molty Form dealership.

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 1 — PRODUCTS  (Catalogue > Products)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Register every product you stock and sell.

How to add a product:
  - Name       : Enter product name
  - SKU/Code   : Optional short code for quick reference
  - Unit Price  : Your selling price (PKR)
  - Cost Price  : What you buy it from supplier for (PKR)

EXAMPLES TO ADD:
  ┌─────────────────────────────────┬──────┬───────────┬────────────┐
  │ Product Name                    │ SKU  │ Sale Price│ Cost Price │
  ├─────────────────────────────────┼──────┼───────────┼────────────┤
  │ Molty Foam Spring Mattress 6x6  │ MF01 │ 45,000    │ 32,000     │
  │ Molty Foam Spring Mattress 5x6  │ MF02 │ 38,000    │ 27,000     │
  │ Molty Foam Spring Mattress 4x6  │ MF03 │ 30,000    │ 21,000     │
  │ Molty Ortho Mattress 6x6        │ MF04 │ 55,000    │ 40,000     │
  │ Molty Foam Single Mattress      │ MF05 │ 18,000    │ 12,500     │
  │ Molty Pillow Standard           │ MF10 │ 2,500     │ 1,600      │
  │ Molty Pillow King Size          │ MF11 │ 3,500     │ 2,200      │
  │ Sofa Foam Sheet (Per Foot)      │ MF20 │ 800       │ 500        │
  └─────────────────────────────────┴──────┴───────────┴────────────┘

  WHY THIS MATTERS: The system automatically calculates your
  profit on every sale using the cost price you enter here.

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 2 — STOCK  (Operations > Stock)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Track how many units you have. Know when to reorder.

--- Adding Stock (when new stock arrives from Molty) ---
  Click "Stock In", fill in:
  - Product    : Molty Foam Spring Mattress 6x6
  - Quantity   : 10
  - Note       : Received from Master Molty — Invoice #4421

  Repeat for each product received in the delivery.

--- Setting Low Stock Alerts ---
  Click the edit (pencil) icon on any product row:
  - Low Stock Threshold : 3
  This means the system will highlight the product in red
  when you have 3 or fewer units remaining.

EXAMPLE OPENING STOCK TO ENTER:
  Product                         | Qty  | Low Alert
  --------------------------------|------|----------
  Spring Mattress 6x6             |  12  |   3
  Spring Mattress 5x6             |   8  |   3
  Spring Mattress 4x6             |  10  |   3
  Ortho Mattress 6x6              |   5  |   2
  Single Mattress                 |  15  |   5
  Standard Pillow                 |  30  |  10
  King Pillow                     |  20  |   5
  Sofa Foam Sheet                 |  50  |  15

--- Stock Out (manual adjustment) ---
  Use "Stock Out" only if a product is damaged/returned
  and needs to be removed from stock without a sale.

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 3 — SALES  (Operations > Sales)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Record every sale made. System auto-deducts stock.

How to record a sale:
  - Date       : Today's date (auto-filled)
  - Product    : Select from dropdown
  - Qty        : How many units sold
  - Price      : Selling price (auto-filled from product, editable)
  - Customer   : Optional — customer name for reference

EXAMPLE SALES TO RECORD:

  Sale 1 — Walk-in customer
    Product  : Molty Foam Spring Mattress 6x6
    Qty      : 1
    Price    : 45,000
    Customer : Ahmed Khan
    → Profit on this sale: 45,000 - 32,000 = PKR 13,000

  Sale 2 — Pillow bundle
    Product  : Molty Pillow Standard
    Qty      : 4
    Price    : 2,500
    Customer : Furniture House Lahore
    → Profit: 4 x (2,500 - 1,600) = PKR 3,600

  Sale 3 — Discounted sale
    Product  : Spring Mattress 4x6
    Qty      : 2
    Price    : 28,000   ← manually reduced from 30,000
    Customer : Regular customer — 2,000 discount given
    → Profit: 2 x (28,000 - 21,000) = PKR 14,000

  TIP: You can change the price per sale for discounts.
  The system uses your entered price (not catalogue price)
  for revenue, but always uses catalogue cost for profit.

STEP 4 — EXPENSES  (Operations > Expenses)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Track all money going OUT of the business.

Categories available:
  UTILITY BILLS          OTHER
  ├── Electricity        ├── Rent
  ├── Internet           ├── Salary
  ├── Water              └── Miscellaneous
  ├── Gas
  └── Phone

EXAMPLE EXPENSES FOR JUNE:

     Amount    : PKR 35,000
     Period    : June 2026
     Date      : June 1, 2026

  2. ELECTRICITY BILL

     Amount    : PKR 8,500
     Date      : June 5, 2026

  3. INTERNET
     Category  : Internet
     Amount    : PKR 3,500
     Date      : June 5, 2026

  4. STAFF SALARY — Salesman

     Category  : Salary
     Employee  : Usman (Helper)         ← required field
     Amount    : PKR 18,000

     Category  : Water
     Amount    : PKR 1,200

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 5 — SUPPLIER LEDGER  (Suppliers > Supplier Ledger)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Track how much you owe each supplier and your
payment history. Keep the relationship transparent.

First, make sure your supplier is added under Settings.

EXAMPLE — Molty Supplier Account:
  Supplier: Master Molty Foam

  Add Entry 1 — Purchase (money owed)
    Type      : Credit (You Owe)
    Amount    : PKR 320,000
    Note      : Invoice #4421 — 10x Spring 6x6
    Date      : June 3, 2026

  Add Entry 2 — Payment made
    Type      : Debit (You Paid)
    Amount    : PKR 200,000
    Note      : Partial payment — bank transfer
    Date      : June 15, 2026

  Add Entry 3 — New stock received
    Type      : Credit (You Owe)
    Amount    : PKR 162,500
    Note      : Invoice #4455 — 5x Ortho 6x6, 15x Pillows
    Date      : June 20, 2026

  → BALANCE: 320,000 - 200,000 + 162,500 = PKR 282,500 still owed

  This balance shows up on the P&L report so you always
  know your outstanding liabilities.

----------------------------------------------------------------
STEP 6 — P&L REPORT  (Reports > P&L Report)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: See if the business is profitable. One-click summary.

Select date range → Click Generate

WHAT YOU SEE:

  [NET PROFIT]  ← Big number at top, green if profit, red if loss
  PKR 47,200

  Revenue          PKR 135,000
  Product Cost     PKR  87,800
  Expenses         PKR  91,000   ← Wait, this doesn't add up?
                                    See BREAKDOWN below.

  WHERE THE MONEY WENT:
  ├── Product Cost               PKR  87,800
  │   ├── Master Molty Foam      PKR  87,800
  ├── Staff Salaries             PKR  43,000
  │   ├── Ali Raza               PKR  25,000
  │   └── Usman                  PKR  18,000
  ├── Rent                       PKR  35,000
  ├── Utility Bills              PKR  13,200
  └── Miscellaneous              PKR     800

  SUPPLIER PAYABLES (what you still owe)
  Master Molty Foam              PKR 282,500

  NOTE: Supplier payables are NOT subtracted from profit —
  they show what you still owe but haven't paid yet.

----------------------------------------------------------------
QUICK DEMO SEQUENCE (for a 10-minute demo)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Follow this order to show the full flow live:

  1. Go to Products → Add 2-3 products with cost & sale price
  2. Go to Stock → Stock In for those products (qty 10 each)
  3. Go to Sales → Record 2 sales, show stock auto-drops
  4. Go to Expenses → Add rent + one salary with employee name
  5. Go to Supplier Ledger → Add a purchase + a payment
  6. Go to P&L Report → Select current month → Generate
     Show the net profit, breakdown, and supplier balance

This demonstrates the complete business cycle in one flow.
