STEP 1 — PRODUCTS  (Catalogue > Products)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Register every medicine and product you stock.

How to add a product:
  - Name       : Brand name + strength/size (be specific)
  - SKU/Code   : Use the company's own product code if available
  - Unit Price  : Your retail selling price (PKR per strip/unit)
  - Cost Price  : Your purchase price from distributor (PKR)

STEP 2 — STOCK  (Operations > Stock)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Know exactly how many strips/units are on your shelf.

--- Adding Stock (when distributor delivers) ---
  Click "Stock In", fill in:
  - Product    : Panadol Tablet 500mg (Strip/10)
  - Quantity   : 50   ← 50 strips received
  - Note       : GlaxoSmithKline — Invoice #GSK-8821

  Repeat for every product in the delivery.

EXAMPLE OPENING STOCK TO ENTER:
  Product                        | Qty  | Low Alert
  -------------------------------|------|----------
  Panadol 500mg Strip            |  80  |   20
  Panadol Extra Strip            |  40  |   10
  Brufen 400mg Strip             |  50  |   15
  Brufen Syrup 100ml             |  20  |    5
  Calpol 120ml                   |  15  |    5
  Augmentin 625mg Strip          |  30  |   10
  Amoxil 500mg Strip             |  40  |   10
  Flagyl 400mg Strip             |  35  |   10
  Cipro 500mg Strip              |  25  |    8
  Nexium 40mg Strip              |  20  |    5
  Motilium Strip                 |  30  |   10
  ORS Sachet                     | 100  |   30
  Scotts Emulsion 200ml          |  15  |    5
  Vitamin C Strip                |  40  |   10
  Ensure 400g                    |  12  |    3
  Disprin Strip                  |  60  |   15

--- Low Stock Alerts ---
  Set the threshold per product. When stock drops below
  that number, it turns red on the stock page.
  Example: Panadol low alert = 20 strips → reorder before
  you run out.

--- Stock Out (damage/expired removal) ---
  If a batch expires or gets damaged, use "Stock Out"
  to remove it without recording it as a sale.
  Note: "Expired batch — returned to distributor"

STEP 3 — SALES  (Operations > Sales)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Record every sale at the counter.

How to record a sale:
  - Date       : Today (auto-filled)
  - Product    : Select medicine
  - Qty        : Number of strips/units sold
  - Price      : Retail price (auto-filled, editable)
  - Customer   : Optional — patient name or "Walk-in"

EXAMPLE SALES:

  Sale 1 — Common OTC purchase
    Product  : Panadol Tablet 500mg
    Qty      : 2   (2 strips)
    Price    : 70
    Customer : Walk-in
    → Revenue: PKR 140 | Profit: 2 x (70-52) = PKR 36

  Sale 2 — Prescription sale (antibiotic)
    Product  : Augmentin 625mg
    Qty      : 1
    Price    : 520
    Customer : Bashir Ahmed
    → Profit: 520 - 390 = PKR 130

  Sale 3 — Multiple items (record each separately)
    First entry:
      Product  : Flagyl 400mg Strip
      Qty      : 1
      Price    : 130
    Second entry:
      Product  : ORS Sachet
      Qty      : 5
      Price    : 35
    → Total for this patient: 130 + 175 = PKR 305

  Sale 4 — Doctor/hospital bulk supply (discounted)
    Product  : Brufen 400mg Strip
    Qty      : 20
    Price    : 70   ← discounted from 80 for bulk
    Customer : City Clinic
    → Revenue: PKR 1,400

  TIP: For walk-in customers you don't know, just leave
  Customer blank or write "Walk-in". Use names only for
  hospitals, clinics, or credit customers.

STEP 5 — SUPPLIER LEDGER  (Suppliers > Supplier Ledger)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Track what you owe each pharmaceutical distributor.

  Pharmacies usually buy on credit — you receive stock today
  and pay the distributor in 30-60 days. This section keeps
  that balance accurate.

First, add your distributors in Settings.
  Common distributors:
  - Fazal Din Pharma
  - United Distributors
  - National Pharma
  - GlaxoSmithKline (direct)

EXAMPLE — Fazal Din Pharma:

  Entry 1 — Stock received (you now owe them)
    Type    : Credit (You Owe)
    Amount  : PKR 185,000
    Note    : Invoice #FD-3301 — June 2 delivery
    Date    : June 2, 2026

  Entry 2 — Payment made
    Type    : Debit (You Paid)
    Amount  : PKR 100,000
    Note    : Cash payment — partial
    Date    : June 10, 2026

  Entry 3 — New delivery received
    Type    : Credit (You Owe)
    Amount  : PKR 92,000
    Note    : Invoice #FD-3389 — June 18 delivery
    Date    : June 18, 2026

  Entry 4 — Final payment
    Type    : Debit (You Paid)
    Amount  : PKR 85,000
    Note    : Bank transfer
    Date    : June 25, 2026

  → BALANCE: 185,000 - 100,000 + 92,000 - 85,000 = PKR 92,000 owed

EXAMPLE — United Distributors:

  Entry 1 — Stock received
    Type    : Credit (You Owe)
    Amount  : PKR 67,500
    Note    : Invoice #UD-1102 — GSK, Abbott items
    Date    : June 5, 2026

  Entry 2 — Payment
    Type    : Debit (You Paid)
    Amount  : PKR 67,500
    Note    : Full payment — cash
    Date    : June 20, 2026

  → BALANCE: PKR 0 — fully cleared

STEP 6 — P&L REPORT  (Reports > P&L Report)
----------------------------------------------------------------
PURPOSE: Is the pharmacy making money? One click to find out.

Select date range (e.g., June 1–30) → Click Generate

SAMPLE REPORT OUTPUT:

  ┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │   NET PROFIT         PKR 64,800         │ ← Green = Profitable
  └─────────────────────────────────────────┘

  Revenue (Medicines sold)    PKR 275,000
  Product Cost (COGS)         PKR  60,000
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  Gross Profit                PKR 215,000
  Total Expenses              PKR 150,200
  ─────────────────────────────────────────
  NET PROFIT                  PKR  64,800

  WHERE THE MONEY WENT:
  ├── Product Cost             PKR  60,000
  │   ├── Fazal Din Pharma     PKR  37,000
  │   └── United Distributors  PKR  23,000
  ├── Staff Salaries           PKR  77,000
  │   ├── Dr. Kamran           PKR  55,000
  │   └── Bilal                PKR  22,000
  ├── Rent                     PKR  45,000
  ├── Utility Bills            PKR  15,000
  └── Miscellaneous            PKR   9,200  (bags + license)

  SUPPLIER PAYABLES (outstanding amounts)
  Fazal Din Pharma             PKR  92,000  ← still to pay
  United Distributors          PKR       0  ← cleared

  NOTE: Profit shown above is BEFORE paying the remaining
  PKR 92,000 to Fazal Din. Your actual cash in hand will be
  lower. The system shows this separately so you can plan.

QUICK DEMO SEQUENCE (for a 10-minute demo)
----------------------------------------------------------------
Follow this exact order to show the full flow:

  1. Products  → Add Panadol 500mg, Augmentin 625mg, ORS Sachet
                 Show cost price vs sale price columns
  2. Stock     → Stock In for those 3 products (30, 20, 100 qty)
                 Show low stock threshold setting (5 for each)
  3. Sales     → Record: 2x Panadol, 1x Augmentin, 5x ORS
                 Show stock numbers drop automatically
  4. Expenses  → Add: Rent PKR 45,000
                 Add: Salary — Dr. Kamran PKR 55,000
                 Show how salary needs employee name
  5. Sup. Ledger → Add Fazal Din: Credit 185,000 (stock arrived)
                   Then: Debit 100,000 (partial payment made)
                   Show running balance = 85,000 still owed
  6. P&L Report → Generate for current month
                  Show net profit, cost breakdown, supplier balance

  KEY POINTS TO HIGHLIGHT DURING DEMO:
  • Salary entries capture WHO got paid — full transparency
  • Supplier ledger shows exactly what you owe, to whom
  • P&L shows profit even if distributor isn't paid yet
  • Stock alerts turn red before you run out
  • Every sale auto-reduces stock — no manual counting

----------------------------------------------------------------
PHARMACY-SPECIFIC TIPS
----------------------------------------------------------------
  ✓ Add products as STRIPS (not individual tablets)
  ✓ Use the Notes field on Stock In for invoice numbers —
    useful if distributor disputes a delivery later
  ✓ Enter expenses on the day they are paid, not due date
  ✓ Record each salary as a SEPARATE entry with staff name
  ✓ Check Stock page weekly — red items need urgent reorder
  ✓ Run P&L monthly to track if margins are holding
